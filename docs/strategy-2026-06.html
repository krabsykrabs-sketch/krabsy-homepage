What's genuinely working

Strengths to protect while scaling

🚀 No-login instant play

Click → playing in 2 seconds on any school device. This is a classroom superpower: teachers can project a game with zero setup, no accounts, no GDPR consent forms. ANTON and sofatutor can't do that.

🎨 A real visual identity

Teal/coral/amber + Fredoka, the crab, consistent start screens, the form-color convention (base=amber, past=teal, participle=coral) that quietly TEACHES across every game. This is brand equity — keep it strict.

⚡ Production velocity

The session model (master + per-game briefs) is shipping a polished game per day with verification baked in. Your moat isn't any single game — it's the FACTORY. No competitor in this niche has one.

🧠 Pedagogy in the loops

Self-dosed studying (Climb's energy, Kitchen's sink), spaced repetition of missed verbs (Valley's Professor Krabsy), teach-on-wrong everywhere. These are honest learning mechanics, not chocolate-covered broccoli.

SEO deep-dive

The alarm 🚨 and the plan

Finding: you are invisible

  • site:krabsy.com returns the OLD domain content: parked "website for sale" pages and a hermit-crab shop (ww1 subdomain ghosts).
  • None of the 50 real URLs surface. New domain + zero links + never submitted = Google hasn't re-crawled.

Fix (week 1, ~2 hours)

  • Google Search Console + Bing Webmaster: verify, submit sitemap, request indexing for the 6 key hubs
  • Check DNS: kill any lingering ww1/parking records
  • Then: patience + links (next slides)

The big on-page lever: programmatic pages

  • 310 verb pages × 2 languages — "went — simple past von go": forms, example sentences, mini-drill embed, link into games. This is EXACTLY how englisch-hilfen & ego4u earn their traffic — yours will be better and faster.
  • Class-level hubs ("Englisch Klasse 5/6/7 üben") — German search behavior is curriculum-anchored; Schlaukopf ranks on these.
  • FAQ blocks on hubs → featured-snippet bait ("Was ist das Past Participle von go?")

Already good — don't touch

hreflang pairs, canonicals, JSON-LD, page speed, mobile. Rare to see this clean on a hobby project.

UX findings

UX: good bones, three gaps

1 · Games ↔ topics are strangers (your own diagnosis — correct)

The games hub and the topic hubs are parallel worlds. A kid drilling prepositions never meets a game; a kid playing Verb Snake never sees the verb table. Fix is the engine refactor (slide 12) + simple cross-links ("Play this topic ▸") on every topic page and a topic badge on every game card.

2 · No "where do I start?"

The DE homepage presents choices but no path. Add one prominent flow: "Klasse wählen → Thema → Üben oder Spielen". Class-anchored entry doubles as the SEO hub structure — one build, two wins.

3 · Progress evaporates

Each game keeps its own localStorage best score; nothing aggregates. A lightweight site-wide profile (still no login — one localStorage profile + optional export code) showing streak, verbs mastered, stars across games would transform return-rate. This is also where a future pet/collection meta-layer docks (the Adopt-Me/Pet-Sim retention pattern we analyzed).

Strategic core · product

The multiplier: a topic-agnostic question engine

Your instinct is exactly right. Today every game inlines its own irregular-verb list — every new topic would mean re-touching 11 games. Invert it:

content/<topic>.json
→
krabsy-questions.js
getQuestions(topic, shape)
→
Games declare supported SHAPES
choice-of-3 · form-chain · gap-fill
→
?topic=prepositions
+ per-topic wrapper pages (SEO)

Why it's THE move

  • Topics × games matrix: every new topic instantly ships with 10+ games; every new game serves every topic.
  • Per-topic game pages ("Prepositions Snake") = more indexed pages, each genuinely different.
  • The drills already read from /data JSONs and games.json already has a topics[] field — the architecture half-exists.

How to sequence it

  • Pilot on ONE single-file game (Verb Flow — newest, cleanest), define the provider interface there.
  • Then sweep the other single-file games (one session each, mechanical).
  • 3D games last — their question UIs are bespoke (blackboard, plates) but consume the same provider.

Honesty section

Risks & uncomfortable truths

1 · Distribution risk dominates everything

The product is ahead of its discoverability by a year. If the SEO/teacher channels don't get real hours (≈30% of total effort), Krabsy stays a beautiful ghost town. The index finding is the proof, not the exception.

2 · ANTON's shadow

Free, loved, installed on school iPads. You won't beat its breadth — don't try. Win the moments it's weak: voluntary play at home, projector minutes, "the fun one the teacher allows on Friday".

3 · Maintenance surface is growing fast

11 games × topics × 2 languages. Without the engine refactor FIRST, topic expansion creates drift and bugs. Sequencing is not optional.

4 · AI shifts search

Kids increasingly ask ChatGPT instead of Google. Partial shelter: games are an *experience* LLMs can't substitute, and curriculum/teacher channels bypass search entirely. But expect long-tail informational queries to erode over years — another reason the games (not the reference pages) are the moat.

5 · Solo-founder energy

The honest failure mode of hobby projects isn't competition — it's month 4. The 90-day plan is deliberately small-batched to keep wins frequent.

Capacity check: ~26 session-days of work in 12 weeks — comfortably inside one-session-per-day cadence, with slack for the playtest-fix loops that real games need.

Final verdict

Is it worth it? Yes — with one condition.

The competition is big in learning and absent in fun. Krabsy's craft already clears the bar; its factory out-ships everyone in the niche. The condition: treat distribution as half the product from this week on. A game a day built nobody-finds-it is a hobby; the same energy split 70/30 with SEO + teachers is a real shot at owning "English üben, aber macht Spaß" for a million German and Spanish schoolkids.
  • Do first: Search Console today · ship the pipeline · engine refactor · verb pages
  • Don't do: new languages, game #12 before the engine, ads, logins
  • Decide in 90 days with data: topics roadmap vs. retention meta-layer (pet/streaks) as the next big bet
